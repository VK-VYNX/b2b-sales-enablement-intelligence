# Maturity

Use this file when the request depends on organizational readiness, sequencing, or the current sophistication of the enablement function.

## Core Principle

Maturity models are only useful when they improve prioritization. Do not use maturity labels as vanity diagnostics. Use them to decide what the organization is actually ready to build, govern, and sustain.

## Maturity Dimensions

Assess maturity across these dimensions:

1. strategic clarity
2. scope and ownership
3. stakeholder alignment
4. intake and governance
5. onboarding and readiness systems
6. coaching and manager enablement
7. content governance
8. methodology adoption
9. technology integration
10. measurement and feedback loops

## Stage Heuristics

### Stage 1: Reactive

- enablement work is request-led
- ownership is unclear
- training dominates the function
- little or no formal measurement exists

### Stage 2: Emerging

- some charter logic exists
- onboarding, training, or content programs are becoming repeatable
- stakeholders recognize enablement but often use it tactically
- reporting is still shallow or fragmented

### Stage 3: Structured

- intake and prioritization are formalized
- programs are multi-lever, not training-only
- manager reinforcement is part of design
- measurement includes outputs and performance levers

### Stage 4: Scaled

- operating model and swimlanes are clear
- hybrid or specialized structures may exist
- content, methodology, and tooling are governed systematically
- measurement connects to pipeline and revenue outcomes

### Stage 5: Systemic

- enablement behaves like a cross-functional operating system
- prioritization is tightly linked to business goals
- field feedback loops are rapid and trusted
- interventions are continuously refined using evidence

## Sequencing Rules

- Fix charter, scope, and intake before scaling programs.
- Fix manager reinforcement before expecting durable behavior change.
- Fix governance before buying more tools.
- Fix measurement logic before making broad ROI claims.
- Scale specialization only after core operating rhythms are stable.

## Diagnostic Questions

- What work is repeatable today versus personality-dependent?
- Which priorities are formally accepted or declined?
- Where does manager inspection exist and where is it absent?
- Which metrics are trusted enough to drive decisions?
- Which systems or rituals break first when demand increases?
