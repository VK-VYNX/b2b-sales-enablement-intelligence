# VYNX Maturity Assessment

Use this file when the user needs the VYNX maturity model specifically for industrial and manufacturing revenue teams.

## Level 1: Reactive Quoting

What it looks like:

- distributors submit RFQs with little qualification
- plant visits are ad hoc and poorly documented
- applications engineering is pulled in late and informally
- proposals are spec-and-price led
- service is engaged after the deal is won
- management reviews volume, not deal quality

To move forward:

- define a basic stage flow
- introduce qualification form and plant-visit checklist
- start weekly review of strategic deals

## Level 2: Structured Response

What it looks like:

- some qualification occurs, but not consistently by distributor
- plant visits happen on larger deals but follow-up is uneven
- applications engineering handoffs exist, but quality varies
- proposals begin to reflect use case, but implementation risk is still weak
- service input is requested on exception
- managers inspect deals inconsistently

To move forward:

- standardize the technical-commercial handoff brief
- introduce proposal-readiness review
- add stalled-deal coding and stage discipline

## Level 3: Managed Strategic Selling

What it looks like:

- high-value deals follow a common process
- plant visits create defined next steps
- applications engineering engagement is triggered by clear rules
- proposal release is reviewed, not automatic
- service is involved in higher-risk deals before release
- managers run a real strategic deal cadence
- distributor performance variance becomes visible

To move forward:

- tier distributors formally
- embed stage controls into CRM and CPQ
- add service-confidence measures and consequence management

## Level 4: Controlled Cross-Functional Motion

What it looks like:

- distributor, direct sales, applications engineering, and service roles are clear
- strategic proposals consistently address commissioning, integration, and support
- poor-fit RFQs are screened out early
- distributor tiers drive support allocation
- monthly leadership dashboard drives action
- loss reasons and stall reasons are reliable enough to manage

To move forward:

- scale the system across regions and segments
- continuously refine rules using performance data
- use tiering and cadence as a management system, not a reporting layer

## Level 5: Industrial Revenue Operating System

What it looks like:

- strategic deal motion is disciplined across channel and direct
- applications engineering capacity is protected and targeted to winnable opportunities
- service credibility is built into sales, not bolted on later
- proposal quality is a managed standard
- plant visits, validation, and reviews produce measurable stage progression
- distributor capability is actively shaped, not passively observed
- leadership decisions are driven by stage leakage, risk, and readiness signals

To sustain:

- audit artifact quality quarterly
- refresh tiering semiannually
- rebalance capacity by segment and distributor performance
- update rules when new failure patterns emerge
