# Stakeholders

Use this file when the request depends on alignment across functions.

## Core Stakeholder Groups

- executive leadership
- sales leadership
- frontline sales managers
- RevOps
- marketing
- product and product marketing
- customer success or post-sales
- sales reps and role-specific audiences

## Stakeholder Management Principles

- Translate enablement work into each stakeholder's language and incentives.
- Clarify decision rights and ownership early.
- Avoid hidden assumptions about success.
- Build credibility through evidence, focus, and follow-through.
- Use governance to prevent randomization by loud requests.
- Make prioritization decisions visible so stakeholders understand why work moves or waits.
- Treat stakeholder management as an operating discipline, not a soft-skill side topic.

## Typical Stakeholder Risks

- Leaders ask for activity rather than outcome-linked interventions.
- Managers are expected to reinforce change but are not enabled to do so.
- Marketing optimizes content creation while sales needs content usability and relevance.
- Product launches create readiness debt if enablement is engaged too late.
- RevOps owns systems but not necessarily behavior adoption.

## Trust-Building Behaviors

- Bring evidence, not only opinions.
- Tie asks to business priorities and seller realities.
- Set boundaries on scope without becoming defensive.
- Translate cross-functional requests into shared success criteria.
- Use structured forums for decisions rather than private negotiation loops.

## Collaboration Measurement

Inspect stakeholder health through:

- alignment and participation quality
- handoff SLAs
- feedback integration rates
- cross-functional initiative execution
- stakeholder sentiment or satisfaction

## Stakeholder Mapping Template

For each stakeholder define:

1. Objective
2. Decision rights
3. Success metric
4. Friction points
5. Required engagement model
6. Communication cadence
