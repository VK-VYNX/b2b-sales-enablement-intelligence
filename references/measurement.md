# Measurement

Use this file to define evidence, KPIs, and review logic.

## Measurement Layers

Separate three layers:

1. Outputs: what enablement directly produces or controls
2. Performance levers: behaviors, adoption, capability, workflow quality
3. Business outcomes: revenue, conversion, cycle time, attainment, retention, expansion

Also situate enablement inside the broader revenue system:

1. enablement inputs
2. sales execution
3. pipeline health
4. sales outcomes
5. board-level GTM outcomes

## Measurement Principles

- Do not confuse activity volume with value.
- Use leading indicators to manage and lagging indicators to validate.
- Measure adoption and behavior change before expecting business impact.
- Connect each KPI to an intervention hypothesis.
- Choose metrics within the function's sphere of control or influence.
- Design evaluation before launch, not after delivery.
- If you cannot explain how a metric informs a decision, remove or downgrade it.

## Metric Families

- onboarding and ramp
- training completion and demonstrated capability
- coaching frequency and quality
- content usage and effectiveness
- methodology adherence
- technology adoption and workflow efficiency
- cross-functional collaboration and intake quality
- seller productivity and pipeline quality
- maturity and operating readiness
- proposal quality and RFQ effectiveness
- distributor and partner execution

## High-Value Distinctions

- Training completion is not readiness.
- Content volume is not content effectiveness.
- Tool access is not adoption.
- Business outcomes without behavior evidence are weak attribution claims.
- Manager inspection is often the missing bridge between training and behavior change.
- Methodology launch is not methodology adoption.
- Collaboration meetings are not collaboration quality.
- AI usage is not workflow impact.
- Plant visits are not opportunity progression unless they create stakeholder movement and disciplined follow-up.
- Quote count is not quote quality.

## KPI Design Questions

- What decision will this metric help make?
- Is this metric owned, shaped, or merely observed by enablement?
- What behavior change should come first?
- What baseline and comparison period exist?
- What segment or cohort matters most?
- What unintended behavior could this metric create?

## Reporting Logic

Present metrics as:

1. objective
2. intervention
3. leading indicators
4. lagging indicators
5. interpretation
6. recommended next action

## Example Measurement Chain

Use chains such as:

- onboarding intervention -> readiness milestone -> pipeline coverage -> quota coverage
- methodology rollout -> manager inspection -> adherence in deals -> stage progression -> win rate or cycle-time impact
- content governance -> trusted asset usage in active deals -> buyer engagement quality -> proposal conversion or deal velocity
