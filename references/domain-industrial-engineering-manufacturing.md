# Domain: Industrial Engineering and Manufacturing

Use this file to anchor recommendations in industrial commercial realities rather than generic B2B assumptions.

## Commercial Characteristics

- long and variable sales cycles
- multiple technical and commercial stakeholders
- technical validation before commitment
- plant visits, demos, pilots, or proofs of value
- channel, distributor, OEM, or integrator involvement
- strong dependence on trust, proof, and execution quality
- post-sale delivery and service quality influencing future revenue

## Common Stakeholders

- plant manager
- operations leader
- maintenance leader
- engineering or applications teams
- procurement
- finance
- quality or compliance stakeholders
- distributor reps
- OEM or system integrator partners

## Common Deal Events

- discovery and site assessment
- technical validation
- specification review
- ROI or business-case discussion
- pilot or demo
- RFQ or RFP response
- pricing and commercial review
- implementation readiness review

## Enablement Implications

- sellers need stronger technical discovery and stakeholder mapping than in simpler transactional environments
- managers need inspection points tied to deal progression, not only activity volume
- content must support technical confidence, operational credibility, and risk reduction
- methodology must fit long-cycle, multi-threaded selling
- onboarding must prepare reps for plant realities, technical objections, and internal coordination
- channel and distributor enablement often determines reach and consistency

## Common Failure Modes

- sales and engineering operate in separate worlds
- proposals answer product questions but not operational risk or value
- RFQ response quality is inconsistent
- distributor messaging and qualification discipline drift
- commercial teams oversell what delivery or service teams cannot support
