# Diagnostics

Use this file to structure root-cause analysis before recommending action.

## Diagnostic Lenses

Check each lens separately:

1. Strategy: Is the enablement objective tied to a business outcome and clear performance gap?
2. Ownership: Who owns the issue, the intervention, and the metric?
3. Leadership: Are sales leaders visibly aligned and reinforcing the change?
4. Manager layer: Are frontline managers equipped and accountable to coach and inspect?
5. Process: Is the desired behavior embedded in workflows, inspection, and systems?
6. Content: Do reps have relevant and usable assets at the right moments?
7. Capability: Do reps know what good looks like and can they execute it?
8. Technology: Do systems support behavior or create friction?
9. Measurement: Is there a feedback loop that distinguishes adoption from impact?

## Symptom-To-Cause Heuristics

- Low content usage may indicate poor relevance, poor findability, low manager reinforcement, weak methodology linkage, or lack of seller trust.
- Weak methodology adoption may indicate abstract training, low manager inspection, poor workflow integration, or contradictory leadership behaviors.
- Poor onboarding outcomes may indicate unclear role outputs, fragmented curriculum, weak ramp metrics, or missing practice and coaching loops.
- Tool underuse may indicate poor workflow fit, weak change management, bad data hygiene, duplicate systems, or no clear owner.
- Stakeholder friction may indicate no intake process, no prioritization framework, unclear charter, or mismatched success criteria.
- Weak program impact may indicate the intervention was designed around sessions instead of target outputs.
- Inconsistent coaching may indicate missing manager rhythm, not missing rep knowledge.
- Weak AI impact may indicate no meaningful use case, poor workflow fit, or no inspection of output quality.
- Methodology fatigue may indicate the rollout measured attendance, not execution quality or manager reinforcement.
- Weak proposal conversion may indicate poor discovery, weak stakeholder mapping, inconsistent technical-commercial handoffs, or proposals that fail to reduce operational risk.
- Weak distributor performance may indicate unclear partner expectations, poor field enablement, low certification quality, or weak deal-review discipline.

## Root-Cause Questions

- What exact performance gap are we trying to close?
- What behaviors or outputs are missing today?
- What happens before and after the failure point?
- Which stakeholder can reinforce or block the behavior?
- Is the issue one of knowledge, skill, motivation, process, tooling, content, management, or incentives?
- What evidence supports the diagnosis?
- What would disconfirm the current hypothesis?

## Output-First Diagnostic Rule

When a problem appears capability-related, first ask:

1. What output should the rep or manager be able to produce?
2. What does good look like?
3. Who inspects that output today?
4. What system reinforces it after launch?

If these are unclear, the issue is probably larger than training.

## Diagnostic Output Template

1. Business problem
2. Observable symptoms
3. Most likely root causes
4. Evidence already available
5. Evidence still needed
6. Highest-leverage interventions
7. Leading indicators
8. Business outcomes expected
