# VYNX Industrial Sales Enablement Intelligence Pack

Use this pack when an agent needs concise, implementation-ready guidance for Industrial Engineering and Manufacturing sales enablement.

## Scope

This pack covers:

- strategy
- diagnostics
- operating model
- stakeholder management
- maturity
- onboarding and readiness
- coaching
- content
- methodology
- technology
- measurement
- distributor and partner enablement
- technical-commercial coordination
- RFQ/RFP and complex proposal support

## Core rules

- Diagnose before prescribing.
- Design around outputs and behaviors, not sessions.
- Treat managers as the main reinforcement layer.
- Separate content, coaching, training, methodology, and technology as different levers.
- Use governance to protect priorities.
- Tie every intervention to expected business impact and measurable signals.
- Adapt every recommendation to long cycles, technical validation, multiple stakeholders, and operational buying realities.

## Diagnostic questions

1. What exact business problem are we trying to solve?
2. What behaviors or outputs are missing?
3. Who owns the issue, intervention, and metric?
4. What managers are expected to reinforce?
5. What process or workflow supports the desired behavior?
6. What content or tools are required at the moment of work?
7. What evidence exists today?
8. What would disconfirm the current diagnosis?
9. Which technical, service, product, or operations stakeholders influence the deal?
10. Which buyer-stage events matter: spec review, demo, trial, plant visit, RFQ, business case, or channel handoff?

## Operating model heuristics

- Use a charter with mission, scope, audience, objectives, roadmap, and KPIs.
- Formalize intake and prioritization early.
- Keep ownership boundaries explicit across enablement, managers, marketing, product, and RevOps.
- Match structure to complexity: centralized first, hybrid later when needed.
- Define sales-to-engineering and sales-to-service handoffs explicitly.
- Treat distributor readiness and field execution as operating-model problems, not only partner-marketing tasks.

## Measurement heuristics

Track:

1. outputs
2. performance levers
3. business outcomes

Never confuse:

- completion with readiness
- launch with adoption
- activity with value
- AI usage with business impact
- quote count with proposal effectiveness
- technical training attendance with field competence

## Maturity heuristics

- Reactive: weak governance, scattered work
- Emerging: some repeatability, still tactical
- Structured: formal programs and better measurement
- Scaled: strong swimlanes and systematic governance
- Systemic: fast learning loops and business-linked prioritization

## Default answer shape

1. Situation
2. Likely root causes
3. Highest-leverage interventions
4. Sequenced rollout
5. Metrics to track
6. Risks and dependencies
